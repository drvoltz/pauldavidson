Research

My group develops digital process twins that connect manufacturing physics, sensing, and inference to predict defect states and structural performance. The emphasis is on validation, uncertainty, and decision-relevance.

Theme A

Digital Process Twins

  • Multi-physics models → quality and performance predictions
  • In situ diagnostics and parameter identification
  • Uncertainty quantification for digital certification

Theme B

AI-Enabled NDI & Interpretation

  • Sparse sensing and physics-informed inference
  • Segmentation, detection, and laminate-level state estimation
  • Linking inspection to structural decisions

Theme C

Thermoplastic Automated Fiber Placement (AFP)

Problem: quality and performance depend on fast, coupled thermo-mechanical interactions during layup.

  • Radiative heating and optical/thermal calibration
  • Tow motion, compaction, and tool interaction
  • Defect formation and consolidation physics

Outputs: predictive models validated with sensing and translated into structural consequences.

  • Process window maps
  • Defect-aware structural performance prediction
  • Closed-loop control concepts

Theme D

Laser Powder Bed Fusion (LPBF)

Problem: microstructure and defects emerge from thermal histories and melt pool dynamics.

  • Thermal modeling + calibration
  • Process monitoring and feature extraction
  • Structure–property inference

Goal: decision-relevant prediction of performance and pathways to digital certification.

  • Process → microstructure → properties
  • UQ for robust decision-making
  • Integration with inspection
