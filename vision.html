Research Vision

My long-term goal is to enable predictive, decision-relevant manufacturing of aerospace structures by linking manufacturing physics to downstream structural behavior through validated digital process twins.

Unifying Thesis

From manufacturing conditions to structural decisions

Digital twins should not stop at process simulation. They must translate manufacturing variability into defect states, microstructure, and ultimately structural performance—with quantified uncertainty—so that design, certification, and control decisions can be made with confidence.

1–3 years

Validated twins

  • Calibration + validation workflows
  • Defect-aware prediction
  • UQ for decision-relevance

3–7 years

Closed-loop manufacturing

  • In situ sensing → inference
  • Model-based control & correction
  • Design–manufacturing integration

7–10 years

Autonomous systems

  • Edge-intelligent twins
  • Holonic/agentic manufacturing
  • ISAM/OSAM directions

How to read this program

  • Composites (AFP): a platform to connect fast multi-physics, sensing, and structural performance.
  • Metal AM (LPBF): a platform to link thermal histories, microstructure, monitoring, and properties.
  • Inspection + AI: a bridge from observable data to latent damage/quality states.
